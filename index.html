The backstory

I spent $180 to confirm what AI had already told me.

I booked a one-hour color analysis in Japantown, went with a friend, tipped $30, and sat in a fluorescent shop front while a professional draped swatches across my shoulders. Her verdict: dark autumn. AI's verdict, two weeks earlier: dark autumn.

"If I could get that result with the right prompt and the right photos, why isn't this more accessible?"

Turns out AI color analysis is frequently wrong — but not because AI can't do it. Because most people don't know how to ask. The right set of photos, paired with a well-constructed prompt, gets you most of the way to a professional-grade result. I know this because I've now done both.

Palette is for women who don't live near a Japantown. Who can't justify the appointment. Who want to know their colors so they can stop second-guessing every purchase, and start building a wardrobe that actually works.
